# Consolidated Risk Assessment

**Audit date:** 2026-07-11  
**Classification:** confirmed findings are based on inspected code/configuration; recommendations without proof of failure are labeled **Hardening**.  
**Effort scale:** XS <1 day; S 1–3 days; M 3–10 days; L 2–6 weeks; XL multi-team/program. Estimates include implementation but not external certification or migration rollout.

## Risk summary

| Severity | Count | Release meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Critical | 9 | direct account/admin/financial/data compromise or unreproducible production state |
| High | 24 | material authorization, money, privacy, integrity, deployment, or availability exposure |
| Medium | 17 | significant reliability/performance/maintainability weakness requiring planned remediation |
| Low | 4 | localized consistency/efficiency concern |
| Informational | 3 | positive assurance boundary or future hardening |

All Critical and High items are production-release blockers unless a security authority documents compensating controls and risk acceptance. Because critical authentication compromise grants administrator APIs, many otherwise admin-only issues become remotely exploitable.

## Risk treatment order

1. **Containment (same day):** restrict public access; disable vulnerable OTP/role/wallet/payment/subscription/notification/upload operations; rotate credentials and revoke sessions.
2. **Integrity recovery (days 1–10):** repair identity and authorization; reconcile payment/wallet/payout/settlement state; preserve and reconcile ledgers; close PII incident.
3. **Reproducibility (weeks 2–6):** migration baseline, automated security/integration tests, dependency upgrades, immutable CI/CD.
4. **Reliability (weeks 4–12):** durable outbox/queues, multi-node sockets/limits/jobs, observability, backup/restore, provider resilience.
5. **Scale/maintainability (quarter):** PostGIS, partitioning, service refactors, generated API contracts, load/chaos certification.

## Residual-risk acceptance

Risk acceptance must identify the owner, expiry, affected assets, measured compensating control, monitoring, and remediation date. Critical authentication, administrator privilege, wallet/payment/payout integrity, committed PII, disabled TLS, migration reproducibility, and zero-test conditions should not be accepted for a public production launch.
